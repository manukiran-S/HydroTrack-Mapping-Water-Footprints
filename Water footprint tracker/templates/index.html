Water Usage Overview

Water Conservation Efforts

Water is essential for all life forms on Earth. With increasing global temperatures and growing populations, our freshwater supply is under immense pressure.

Simple actions—like fixing leaks, using low-flow fixtures, harvesting rainwater, and choosing native plants for landscaping—can drastically reduce personal and community water footprints.

Global initiatives such as the UN’s Sustainable Development Goal 6 (Clean Water and Sanitation) aim to ensure access to safe and affordable drinking water for all. You too can contribute by spreading awareness and adopting conservation habits in daily life.

Annual Water Usage

According to global data, agriculture consumes approximately 70% of freshwater resources, while industry and households share the remaining portion. Efficient irrigation techniques like drip irrigation can reduce agricultural water demand.

Domestic usage also plays a major role—washing machines, toilets, and showers are top household water consumers. Modern appliances and mindful usage habits can drastically cut down consumption.

Using this tracker, you can monitor your daily, monthly, and yearly water usage, helping you make informed decisions and set conservation goals tailored to your lifestyle.
